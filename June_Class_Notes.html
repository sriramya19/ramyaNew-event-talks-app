Chapter I

The Multiplication of Miracles

It is taught by Sri Bhagavan that the nature of divine miracles is non-linear and cumulative. Miracles do not diminish when shared; rather, they expand. Whenever a devotee hears, reads, or speaks about a miracle, that divine event acts as a seed within their consciousness. Therefore, the sharing of miracles is a core practice of the Dharma.

When you witness or learn of a miracle, you must observe it with utmost attention and sacredness. This intent aligns your own mind to receive corresponding grace. The transformation sharings are not mere testimonies; they are templates of neurobiological changes. As your brain adapts to these details, it is prepared for the global consciousness transition.

Chapter II

Chronicles of the Miraculous

Witnesses in the June M&M class shared profound events illustrating divine intervention across distances, physical bodies, and limitations.

The Savior of the Sea

A skeptic wife, who had doubted her husband's faith in Sri Amma Bhagavan for 18 years, recalled a forgotten event from a decade prior during a session in the presence of Sri Bhagavan:

"I was drowning in a turbulent, raging sea ten years ago when a fair-skinned lady appeared and pulled me onto the shore using only her little finger. I wrote it off as a hallucination. But during meditation, the lady looked at me and said, 'Who else but me could have saved you with a little finger?' It was Sri Amma."

Medical Grace in Honnavar

Dr. Mukta Ovdani, a gynecologist from Karnataka, related miracles she experienced during her 21 years of service: after a severe abdominal injury and a fracture of the spine, she was instantly cured during an online Homa. Later, while treating a patient with a breech baby, she had a vision of tiny Bhagavan entering the womb to safely rotate the baby's head down for delivery. Additionally, despite never studying Sanskrit, she composed over 5,000 slokas and devotional tracks under divine influence.

Chapter III

Dialogues with the Master

During the question and answer session, Sri Bhagavan resolved core doubts regarding familial grace, the nature of peak states, and the importance of relationships.

Q: Can one family member's benediction bring breakthroughs for the entire family?
Yes, if a single member receives a blessing, it will naturally flow to the rest of the household.
Q: Sri Bhagavan teaches that respecting women brings immediate grace. How is this possible?
In ancient times, the Divine was respected as female. Re-establishing respect and love for your mother, wife, and daughter directly changes your brain functionality, enabling divine downloads.
Q: What is a Peak State?
A Peak State is when the physical brain becomes completely quiet. In this state, solutions are downloaded from other realms. Ramanujan received mathematical downloads from Goddess Namagiri, and Einstein accessed laws of physics this way.
Q: What is the difference between detachment and indifference?
Detachment is seeing that you are everything. Indifference is trying to escape your family or the world. True detachment is built on Oneness.
