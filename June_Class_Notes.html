The Power of Reading, Hearing & Sharing Miracles

Sri Bhagavan teaches that miracles multiply when they are read, heard, and shared. When we are shown a miracle, we must observe it carefully to draw it into our own lives. Transformation sharing prepares the listener's brain for changes that qualify them for the global transition.

Miracle & Transformation Sharing

Miracle Divine Protection

The Guardian Angel of the Sea

A testimonial shared by the wife of a skeptic. While her husband was a devoted follower of Sri Amma Bhagavan, she remained logical and doubtful for 18 years. Her perspective changed completely after a vision and a recollected memory.

"Ten years ago, I fell into a turbulent sea and was drowning. A fair-skinned lady pulled me out of the deep sea using just a little finger. I thought I hallucinated and forgot it. Now, years later in the presence of Sri Bhagavan, I lay down and saw the lady. She turned, smiled, and said: 'Who else but me could have pulled you out of the turbulent sea with a little finger?' It was Sri Bhagavathy Padmavathi Amma."

This experience transformed her from a skeptic to one deeply, passionately, and unrestrainedly in love with the Divine.

Questions & Answers with Sri Bhagavan

Q&A Family Breakthroughs

Can one family member's benediction bring Bhakti, surrender, and mukti to the rest of the family?

Yes, it is entirely possible. If one family member receives it as a benediction, that is how it will flow to the other family members.

Q&A Respecting Women

Sri Bhagavan teaches that if we respect women, we will receive grace immediately. How do we make this a reality?

Respecting women alters brain functioning. In ancient times, God was viewed as a female (a Goddess) rather than a male, and females were highly respected. Historically, as male egos sought to dominate, they suppressed the female deities—even the ancient Jewish/Christian God, Yahweh, had a wife whose statues were eventually broken and buried.

To receive grace, you must change this condition by learning to respect, value, and love the women in your life—your mother, your wife, your daughter. When this happens, your brain begins to function differently, facilitating divine downloads.

Q&A Peak States

What exactly is a "Peak State" and what is the experience?

To understand a peak state, one must look at quantum physics and the brain. Essentially, a peak state is when the physical brain becomes completely quiet.

When the brain is quiet, it connects to other realms where deities exist. In this state, solutions are downloaded directly. Examples include:

  • Ramanujan: He went into peak states while sleeping, where the Goddess Namagiri downloaded complex mathematical formulas directly into his mind.
  • Albert Einstein & Isaac Newton: Both had the capacity to quiet their brains, allowing the download of the laws of physics and gravity.
Q&A Enlightenment Paths

How do we proceed towards Enlightenment?

The path to enlightenment in the Dharma involves sequential courses:

  1. Participating in the Bhakti Dhyana programs and Homas.
  2. Completing the Art of Fulfillment (11-day program).
  3. Completing the Art of Transformation (11-day program).
  4. Completing the Art of Enlightenment (11-day program).
Q&A Divine Bonding

What is the best way to develop a strong bond with Sri Amma Bhagavan?

The bond with the Divine is a reflection of your earthly relationships. If you take good care of your parents and keep your relationships healthy (with your spouse, children, and parents), you will automatically receive a strong bond with the Divine.

Q&A Detachment vs Indifference

What is the difference between indifference and detachment?

Detachment is when you see that you are everything; it is built on Oneness. When you realize Oneness, detachment is natural.

Indifference occurs when you try to escape the world or become something else, often abandoning your responsibilities or family. True detachment does not require escaping to the mountains.

Q&A True Bhakti

What is real Bhakti and how does it grow?

Bhakti often starts as "bogus" or transactional Bhakti (asking for favors). However, as the brain quiets down and enters a peak state, real Bhakti arises. You realize that you are not the doer, and a natural state of gratitude and devotion flows.

Q&A Kalki Dharma vs Oneness

What is the difference between Kalki Dharma and the Oneness Movement?

While both share the same foundation and premises, the focus differs:

  • Kalki Dharma: Entirely Sri Amma Bhagavan centered. It relies on grace, and Deeksha is the main vehicle.
  • Oneness: Focused on the state of Oneness itself, relying heavily on self-effort and general spiritual processes.
